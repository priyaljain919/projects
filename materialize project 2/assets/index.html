Ever Wondered Why Atal Bihari Vajpayee is One of India's Most Celebrated Prime Ministers?

His life was all about contributing to India's progress.

Advertisement

Arguably, Atal Bihari Vajpayee is India's most celebrated Prime Minister. Even though he failed to form a government after the 1996 General Elections, the man, content in his mind to become the Prime Minister stood against all the odds to win the elections in 1998 and become the first non-Congress prime minister to serve full-term in the office.

Born in December 1924, he was one of those leaders who saw India's transformation from British colonial rule to partition and its emergence as the modern nation that would become the world's fastest-growing economy in the future.

Let's honour life of (now) 93-year-old, Atal Bihari Vajpayee by looking at the achievements that make him one of India's most celebrated prime minister.

Advertisement

Indian became a nuclear weapon state under his leadership.

"India is now a nuclear weapons state. We have the capacity for a big bomb now. Ours will never be weapons of aggression."- Atal Bihari Vajpayee.

The Western countries condemned Shri Vajpayee, but for our nation, he was a hero. He successfully made India a nuclear weapon state in the year 1999.

Advertisement

Atal Bihari Vajpayee is the man behind India's success in telecom industry.

A decade after Rajiv Gandhi left the office, India's growth in the telecom industry increased from 0.6% (1989) to 2.8% (1999). When BJP introduced New Telecom Policy (NTP), it led to the rise in telecom sector from 3% (1999) to 70% market penetration until 2012.

He taught us, education is for everyone.

Atal Bihari Vajpayee laid the foundation of Right to Education in India by launching the Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan. It became hugely popular as Education for All Movement.

He created world-class highways in India to improve the transportation of the nation.

In 1998, Atal Bihari Vajpayee unveiled the National Highway Development Project(NHDP) to construct world-class highways in India and improve the transportation within the nation. The NHDP represents 49,260 km of roads and highways.
